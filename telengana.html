Telengana:

Food:

Popular Telangana curry dishes (known as Koora) include Boti (derived from mutton) and Phunti
Koora made out of Red Sorrel leaves. Potlakaya pulusu, or Snake gourd stew is one of the daily
staple dish. Many Telangana dishes are altered as per their own taste but the root ingredients are
similar.

Vegetation:

The forests, covering about one-fourth of the land area, consist of both moist deciduous and dry
savanna vegetation; teak, rosewood, wild fruit trees, and bamboo are plentiful. Elsewhere in the state, neem (which produces an aromatic oil),
banyan, mango, and pipal (or Bo; Ficusreligiosa) are among the common trees.

Climate:

Telangana is a semi-arid zone and has a predominantly hot and dry climate. The areas covered
by the Deccan Plateau are characterized by hot summers with relatively mild winters. Summers start in March,
and peak in May with average high temperatures in the 42 °C (108 °F) range.
