Tripura:

Food:

Apong, a local drink is also made from millet rice. * Muya Awandru is a Tripuri
food item which contains bamboo shoot, rice flour and Berma. * The Kosoi Bwtwi
is made of Beans and Berma. * Muya bai Wahan is another food item of bamboo shoots,
jackfruit, papaya, and pork.

Vegetation:

The interspersion of bamboo and cane forests with deciduous and evergreen flora
is a peculiarity of Tripura's vegetation. Grasslands and swamps are also present, particularly
in the plains. Herbaceous plants, shrubs, and trees such as Albizia, Barringtonia,
Lagerstroemia and Macaranga flourish in the swamps of Tripura.

Climate:

The climate of Tripura exhibits a strong seasonal rhythm. The
state is characterised by a warm and humid tropical climate with five distinct seasons, namely, spring,
summer, monsoon, autumn and winter. Spring starts from late mid-February & continues
till midMarch. ... Humidity is generally high throughout the year.
